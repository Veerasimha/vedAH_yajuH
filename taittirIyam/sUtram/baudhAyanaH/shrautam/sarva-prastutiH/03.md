तृतीयः प्रश्नः

## ०१ 
<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अग्नीन्पुनराधास्यमानो भवति
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

(Now about one who) is going to reset his fires. 
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

तदाहुः कृतयजुः सम्भृतसम्भारो भवति न सम्भृत्याः सम्भारा न यजुः कर्तव्यमित्यथो खलु सम्भृत्या एव सम्भाराः कर्तव्यं यजुः पुनराधेयस्य समृद्ध्या इति
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

They say, "(the Adhvaryu) had (already) recited the yajus (pertaining to the setting) and had procured the substances. (Therefore, at the resetting) the substances need not be procured, nor is the yajus to be recited. (This is not correct). The substances are indeed to be procured and the yajus (also) is to be recited for the accomplishment of the resetting."[^1-123]  

[^1-123]: TS. I. 5. 2.4  

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

स उपकल्पयते पुनर्निष्कृतँरथं पुनरुत्स्यूतं वासः पुनरुत्सृष्टमनड्वाहं दर्भकुलायं तिस्रो दर्भमयीर्विसूर्मिकाः
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

He (the sacrificer) procures a refurnished chariot, resewn garment, a bull who is let loose again, aweb of darbha-grass and three handfuls of darbha-grass. 
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

The substances pertaining to the setting of fires are well-known. 
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सा याषाढ्याः पौर्णमास्याः पुरस्तादमावास्या भवति सा सकृत्संवत्सरस्य पुनर्वसुभ्याँसम्पद्यते तस्यामादधीतेति
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

The new-moon day which precedes the full-moon of the month of Aṣādha coalesces with the Punarvasu constellation only once in a year; one should reset on that day.  
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

तस्या उपवसथेऽरण्योरग्नीन्समारोह्योदवसाय मथित्वाग्नीन्विहृत्याग्नेयमष्टाकपालं निर्वपति वैश्वानरं द्वादशकपालमग्निमुद्वासयिष्यन्निति
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

On the Upavasatha of that day, having consigned the fires into the kindling woods, having gone away, having churned the fire and having spread out fires (the Adhvaryu) who is going to dispose of the (old) fire pours out paddy for (a cake on) eight potsherds for Agni and another on twelve potsherds for Vaiśvānara Agni. 
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सा प्रसिद्धेष्टिः सन्तिष्ठतेऽथ तदानीमेवाद्भिरग्नीन्समुक्ष्य ब्रह्मौदनँ श्रपयित्वोपवसत्यथ प्रातराग्न्याधेयिकं कर्म तायत एतावदेव नाना
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

That Iṣṭi comes to a conclusion with the established procedure. Subsequently, having effused the fires with water and having cooked Brahmaudana (the sacrificer) observes the Upavasatha vow. Next morning the rite pertaining to the setting of fires starts. This much is different: 
</details>

</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

<details><summary>English</summary>

One sets up the Gārhapatya with darbha-blades; the Anvāhāryapacana with darbha-blades; the Āhavanīya with darbha-blades. When he sets up the Gārhapatya, he adds the Sarparājñī verses[^2-123] in recitation.  

[^2-123]: TS. I. 5.3 
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

Four with regard to the Gārhapatya: "Thou art the earth in depth, sky in width, mid-region in greatness. In thy lap, O goddess Aditi, I place Agni the food-eater for the eating of food-The spotted bull has come and reached again the mother and the father, faring to the heaven-Thirty abodes shine forth. Speech took resort (in them) for the sun. Do thou leave away (O fire, our harshness). Carry (our oblations) day by day-The brilliance of this (fire) moves within (heaven and earth) expirating and inspirating. The great (sun) has illumined the heaven.[^1-125] Three with regard to the Āhavanīya: "O Fire, if I excited, disposed of thee through rage or misfortune; may that (disposal) of thine be recompensated. We reillumine thee. That lustre of thine, disposed by me through rage which is dispersed on the earth, may Ādityas, Viśve Devas and Vasus recompensate-May the adorable light rejoice in clarified butter; may it unite this scattered sacrifice. May Br̥haspati accomplish it for us. May Viśve Devas rejoice here."[^2-125]  

[^1-125]: TS. I. 5.3.1  

[^2-125]: TS. I. 5.3.2

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

<details><summary>English</summary>

(The second) "We choose Agni as the messenger; O Agni, may Tanūnapāt consume the clarified butter" (The third) "Agni is enkindled together with Agni; O Agni, may the Iḍās consume the clarified butter." (The fourth) "May Agni dispel the evils, O Agni; may Barhis consume the clarified butter." 
</details>

This is the view of some teachers. They should be agna āyūṁṣi pavase and agne pavasva svapā."[^6-125] This is the view of some teachers.

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

यत्पवमानं तत्सोमरूपम्पङ्क्त्यौ हविषोऽग्ने तमद्याधा ह्यग्न इत्यथ पुनरूर्जा सह रय्येत्यभितः पुरोडाशमाहुती जुहोत्यग्निं प्रति स्विष्टकृतं निराहाभिष्टे अद्य एभिर्नो अर्कैरिति संयाज्ये

<details><summary>English</summary>

The puronuvākyā-yājyā of the principal offering are in Paṅkti metre, namely, agne tam adya..[^7-125] and adhā hy agne.[^7-125] The Adhvaryu offers two oblations (of clarified butter) on both sides of the offering of the cake (that is, before and after) respectively with the verses, "Return with strength, return, O Agni, with food and life. Guard us again on all sides. Return with wealth, O Agni, swell with the stream gaining all on every side."[^8-125] The Hotr recites loudly with regard to Svistakṛt Agni. The puronuvākyā-yājyā are ābhiṣṭe adya[^9-125] and ebhir no arkaiḥ...[^9-125] respectively.

[^8-125]: TS.I. 5.3.3

</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

He would receive food for his sustenance."[^1-127] May the divine Barhis accept the gift of wealth for the desirer of wealth (ie. the sacrificer). “(The second Anūyaja-yājyā is)” We the desirers of wealth exclaim the desire-yielding praise of Agni residing in the heaven (as the sun).[^2-127] May god Narāśamsa accept the gift of wealth for the desirer of wealth (i.e. the sacrificer).”

[^2-127]: R̥V. V. 13.2

Do thou be thinking and create divine being."[^3-183] He puts into the Barbis the darbha-blades which remain, with the formula, "Thou art the filler; fill me with offspring and cattle."
